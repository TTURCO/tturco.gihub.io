About me:

I am a PhD student at the ENES BIOACOUSTICS RESEARCH LAB, one of the main research centers for the science of animal and human sound signals. Rooted in ethology (study of behavior), ENES has a long tradition in ecology (impact of abiotic and biotic environments on the evolution of communications; bioacoustic tools for the assessment of biodiversity). I am mainly interested in fish/invertebrates, trophic relationships and environmental conservation. I have a passion for electronics and its application to the development of innovative tools for research.

My research work combines aspects from physics, computer science, ecology and ethology. I teach R programming to students of master 1 ecology ethology and chemical ecology

MY RESEARCH:

MAIN PROJECT:

Anthropogenic (man-made) noise is one of the most pervasive symptoms of human activities. It is expected to increase worldwide and ecological impact remains unclear, especially in freshwaters. Through the POLLUSON project, we use passive acoustic monitoring to quantify nautical activity and understand the phenology and nature of noise pollution on the largest natural lake in France. We also use a key organism-targeted approach to study behavioral responses and test whether these individual responses spill over into the community and disrupt ecosystem processes.
